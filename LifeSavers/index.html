Blood Types

Simply put, a blood type can be defined as a red blood cell that contains a specific surface antigen (surface marker). Differences in human blood are due to the presence or abscence of certain protein molecules called antigens and antibodies. The antigens are located on the surface of the red blood cells and the antibodies are in the blood plasma.

A blood type can be classed as positive or negative,this refers to a protein (called Rhesus) found on the covering of the red blood cells. A person's blood is Rhesus positive if the protein is present and Rhesus negative if the protein is absent.
